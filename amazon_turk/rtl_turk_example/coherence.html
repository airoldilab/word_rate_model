Instructions and guidelines:

You will be presented with a list of topics, and the task is to rate if each topic is coherent, if it describes a concept/subject or simply 'makes sense'. Note that a topic is a list of words.

The following scale should be used for scoring:

  • 3 = Very coherent; the list of words collectively describes a clear concept; it 'makes sense'
  • 2 = Mildly coherent; the words seem to describe a concept/subject, but it is not very clear (or not as clear as '3')
  • 1 = Incoherent; the list of words does not 'make sense'

'2' is between '1' and '3' in terms of coherence. Often, with '3', it is easy to think of a concept to summarise the topic. With '1', it is difficult.

Examples of '3' (very coherent):

  • court law lawyer judge case (topic is about 'law')
  • rate market percent price economy (topic is about 'economy')
  • music song band rock album (topic is about 'music')

Examples of '1' (incoherent):

  • williams letter wrote murphy koch
  • globe photos frank mass bear
  • north south carolina korea korean
